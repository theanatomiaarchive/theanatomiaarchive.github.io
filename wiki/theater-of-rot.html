The Theater of Rot

EXHIBIT A
Classification Biological / Spontaneous Decay
Acquisition Date October 14, 1892
Containment Sealed Bell Jar (Atmospheric-Lock)
Status Active Observation

Observer's Notes

Found within the cellar of a derelict infirmary, this specimen defies the standard timeline of organic expiration. Within the glass, the flora and tissue undergo a perpetual state of "rapid cellular collapse," yet never reach a state of total dissolution. It is a performance of ending that never quite finishes. Observers have reported a faint, high-pitched vibration emanating from the glass when the room is silent. It is suggested that the rot is not merely biological, but a localized temporal distortion. "To look into the jar is to see a thousand winters happen in a second, over and over again."
