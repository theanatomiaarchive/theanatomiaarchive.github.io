The Surveyor's Plumb

EXHIBIT I
Classification Non-Euclidean Measurement
Origin Jenks/Bixby Allotment (1921)
Containment Lead-lined Box / Sealed
Status Active / Kinetic

Observer's Notes

A teardrop-shaped weight of high-density lead encased in etched copper. The suspension cord is composed of braided human hair and silver-wrapped silk thread. Recovered from a collapsed cellar near the 1921 flood zones, the object was found suspended in a state of impossible stillness despite heavy local drafts. Analysis indicates the Plumb does not respond to standard gravitational pull. It instead deflects toward localized "unresolved" organic matter. In current testing within the Lénnistree, the weight maintains a constant 45-degree angle toward the primary collection vault, straining against the cord with enough force to cause audible clicking against the interior of its lead-lined containment. "It does not map the soil. It maps the gaps where the soil should be."
