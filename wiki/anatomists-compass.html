The Anatomist's Compass

EXHIBIT D
Classification Industrial / Bio-Navigation
Acquisition Date June 19, 1898
Containment Lead-lined Box
Status Calibrated to Local Silt

Observer's Notes

Originally a maritime tracker, this device has been heavily modified with copper wiring and animal sinew. The needle does not point North. Instead, it reacts to concentrated bio-electric pulses. It is used by Archive field agents to locate "unstable masses" within the Blackwood Silt. The needle has been observed to spin wildly when in the presence of a Displacement event. "True North is a comfort for sailors. We require a compass for the soul."
