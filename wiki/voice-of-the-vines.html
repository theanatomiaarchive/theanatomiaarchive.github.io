The Voice of the Vines

EXHIBIT C
Classification Biological / Mimicry
Acquisition Date March 03, 1901
Containment Humidified Vitrine
Status Constant Pulsation

Observer's Notes

A cluster of calcified root structures harvested from the Blackwood marshes. Analysis reveals a structure closer to human vocal cords than standard plant xylem. When the specimen is exposed to open air, it begins a low, rhythmic thrumming. The vibration is not random; it appears to be a form of echolocation designed to mirror the heartbeats of those standing within a six-foot radius. "It does not speak, yet it knows the rhythm of your blood."
