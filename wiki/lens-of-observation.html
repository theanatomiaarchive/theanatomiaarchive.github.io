The Lens of Observation

EXHIBIT B
Classification Industrial / Spectral Optics
Acquisition Date November 22, 1894
Containment Felt-lined Brass Casing
Status Fully Functional

Observer's Notes

A brass-housed optical device recovered from the workshop of a disgraced clockmaker. Unlike standard lenses, this glass has been treated with a proprietary solution of silver nitrate and crushed sea-glass. When viewed through the lens, the atmosphere takes on a viscous, oil-slick quality. It allows the user to see "lingering miasma"—the static-like residue left behind by sudden emotional or physical trauma. "The world is crowded with things we were never meant to see. This lens simply peels back the skin of the air."
