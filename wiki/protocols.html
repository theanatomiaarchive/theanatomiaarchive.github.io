DIRECTIVE 18.92.B

The Masqueraide Protocols

Handling Guidelines for Authorized Observers of the Anatomia Archive.

I. The Principle of Selective Description

No artifact within the Ledger shall be described in its absolute entirety. To define a vessel, a specimen, or a site with 100% accuracy is to provide it a conceptual exit from the Archive.

Always leave one detail unwritten. The blank space is the containment.

II. The Saturday Reconciliation

Artifacts (The Objects) and Field Logs (The Narratives) must be categorized separately. When a story and its corresponding object occupy the same mental space for more than 72 hours, the object may begin to "manifest" the fiction.

Saturdays are reserved for the stories. Weekdays are for the steel and glass of the exhibits.

III. The Tax of Sight

Prolonged observation of the digital Ledger induces a spiritual miasma on the retinas. For every hour spent reviewing the Unabridged Ledger, the observer must spend ten minutes in total sensory deprivation (darkness).

If the red scanner beam appears to turn blue, cease observation immediately.

IV. The Shadow Clause

Should any text within a Ledger entry change while you are actively reading it, do not attempt to correct it. Close the connection, vacate the immediate area, and wait for the scanner to pass over the entry three full cycles.

Violation of these protocols may result in permanent Specimen Status.
